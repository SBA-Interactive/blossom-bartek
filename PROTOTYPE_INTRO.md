# Blossom — Website Concept Walkthrough

Alright, so let me walk you through the first website concept we've put together for Blossom. We're creating three prototypes for this project overall, but this is the one we wanted to start with and get your thoughts on.

The brand is called Blossom, and what they do is actually really interesting. They take fruit peels — I'm talking orange peels, lemon peels, all that citrus stuff that normally gets thrown away — and they transform these leftover peels into actual perfumes. Real fragrances you can wear. That's their whole thing.

Now when you think about the perfume industry, most of what's out there on the market is loaded with chemicals and synthetic ingredients. It's not great for your skin, it's not great for the environment, it's just kind of accepted as normal. Blossom is the complete opposite. Their products are natural, eco-friendly, and here's the thing that really makes them different — they're safe enough for kids age ten and up. That's kind of a huge deal when you think about it. Most fragrances out there, you wouldn't dream of giving them to a teenager, let alone someone younger. These ones smell fresh and modern and fruity, but they don't have all those harsh chemicals you'd find in a typical department store scent.

Their target audience is Gen Z, specifically people aged eighteen to twenty-four. These are the consumers who are really driving the demand for clean beauty products. They're looking for things that are natural, that align with their values, that feel authentic and honest. They care about sustainability. They care about what's actually in the products they put on their body. They want to make responsible choices without having to sacrifice quality or style. But here's the thing — they also want something that smells good. They don't want to feel like they're sacrificing freshness and modernity for the sake of being natural. They want something that smells like a real fragrance, something that feels current and relevant, not like walking through some hippie health food store where everything smells like patchouli and sandalwood. That's exactly the balance Blossom is trying to hit. They want to be sustainable and natural and eco-friendly without being preachy about it or turning off the very audience they're trying to reach.

The brief we received from the client was pretty clear and well thought out. They wanted a website that felt clean and premium, something that highlighted all the natural and eco-friendly aspects of the brand without being overwhelming about it or preaching to people. But beyond just the general vibe, they had some very specific concerns about common mistakes they see other websites make.

First, they were worried about complicated designs that confuse visitors and make it hard to figure out what to do next. They wanted simple and intuitive. Second, they specifically mentioned hidden buy buttons — you know how some sites make it surprisingly difficult to actually find the button that lets you purchase something? They wanted to avoid that completely. Third, low-quality images that make the brand look cheap and knock it down a notch. Fourth, websites that don't work properly on mobile, which is insane in 2024 but somehow still happens. Fifth, unclear product descriptions that leave people confused about what they're actually buying. And sixth, complicated checkout flows that are such a pain in the ass that people just abandon their carts and leave. Basically, they wanted us to solve all of these problems before they even had the chance to become issues. So we set out to build something that is simple, easy to use, and laser-focused on one primary thing: getting people to actually complete a purchase. Everything else is secondary to that goal.

So let's get into what we actually put together for the website. We've got five main pages that make up the core structure.

The home page serves as the landing experience. This is where people first land when they visit the site, so we made sure it immediately introduces the brand and shows featured products right away so visitors get a sense of what Blossom is all about in those first few seconds. It's got a hero section, some featured products, and clear calls to action that guide people deeper into the site.

The About page tells the story. This is where we explain the sustainability mission and really dig into why this whole fruit peel thing matters. We wanted to give people a real understanding of what makes Blossom different and why they should care about the product beyond just the smell.

The Products page is essentially the catalog. This is where people browse and actually see the fragrances Blossom offers. Each product gets its own detail page with descriptions, pricing, and all the information someone would need to make a decision.

The Pricing page covers cost information so people know exactly what to expect before they commit to buying. No surprises, no hidden fees, just clear straightforward pricing.

The Contact page is there so people can reach out easily if they have questions, want to ask for more information, or need support with something. Every business needs a way for customers to get in touch.

Now beyond just the pages, the website has the actual functionality that a real e-commerce site needs to have to work.

We've got user accounts so people can create a profile, log in, and manage their orders. That's pretty standard nowadays but essential for building a relationship with customers.

We've got a shopping cart and checkout system so people can actually complete a purchase. This is obviously the most important part of the entire site from a business perspective. We made sure this flow is as smooth and straightforward as possible.

We've got contact forms for when someone has a question or needs support. Simple and easy to use.

We've got newsletter signup so the brand can build an email list for marketing and promotions. This is huge for e-commerce businesses because email is still one of the most effective channels for driving sales.

We've designed it to be fully responsive, which means it works on mobile just as well as it does on desktop. This is absolutely essential for this audience because Gen Z primarily browses on their phones. If the mobile experience sucks, you've already lost them.

The site supports two languages for broader reach, so they can actually serve a wider audience beyond just one language group.

It's built to comply with GDPR because that's just responsible handling of customer data. You can't just collect people's information willy-nilly anymore.

The site itself is brand new, not a redesign. They don't have a domain yet, so that's something we'll handle down the road when we're closer to launch.

And we built search engine optimization in from the start, which means we structured everything properly so they'll actually be discoverable on Google once this goes live. Nobody's going to find the site if you don't build that in from the beginning.

Now let's talk about the visual direction because that's a huge part of what makes this work.

We went with a clean and minimal visual style. I want to be really clear about that. It's stripped back, there's no clutter, nothing overwhelming or flashy or trying too hard. Every single design choice we made reinforces the idea that Blossom is a natural and straightforward brand. We didn't want anything that felt over-designed or corporate or fake. Just clean, honest, simple.

For the colors, we used tones that reflect the fruit origin of the products. Think soft natural colors, citrusy vibes, nothing that feels synthetic or aggressive. We wanted the colors to feel like an extension of the product itself.

For typography, we went with clean and readable fonts. It needed to feel premium but at the same time accessible for that Gen Z audience. Not too fancy, not too basic. Just right.

For messaging, we kept it consistent across the entire site: natural, eco-friendly, safe for ages ten and up, fresh modern scents. Those are the key points that we hammer home everywhere because that's what makes Blossom different from every other fragrance brand out there.

And here's the big one — we made absolutely certain that the buy buttons are obvious everywhere on the site. No hunting around trying to figure out how to actually purchase. No hidden tricks. The entire checkout process is designed to be quick and painless. This was honestly one of the strongest requests from the client. Don't hide the buy button. Make it easy. So it's prominent on every product page with clear calls to action.

To make sure we were on the right track, we looked at who the client considers their competition and who they admire in the market. They specifically mentioned Aesop, Le Labo, Herbivore Botanicals, Glossier, and L'Occitane. These brands are all doing something right, and they all have one thing in common. They feel authentic. They're not over-designed. They tell real stories. They feel premium but not elitist, not like they're looking down on you. That's exactly the feeling we're going for with Blossom. Someone visiting this website should feel like they're dealing with a real brand with real values, not some corporate fragrance company trying to trick them into buying something. We wanted that same honesty and genuineness.

So that's where we are now. We've got this first concept built out and we're looking for feedback from the team. Once we get your thoughts on this, we'll iterate and refine based on what everyone thinks.

After that, we'll finalize all of the actual content — the words, the images, all the pieces that make a website feel complete and polished. Then we'll move into actual development to get everything wired up so the site can actually function.

One thing I want to mention is that the client has already mentioned they want to scale this business. They expect to grow, add new scents, build out new product lines. So we've built the site in a way that can actually grow with them over time. It's not just some basic setup that's only going to work for right now. It's built to handle expansion.

What we've put together here is a clean, conversion-focused e-commerce experience. It reflects everything Blossom stands for: natural, sustainable, premium, and accessible all at the same time. It's designed specifically for that Gen Z audience, it works great on mobile where they'll be spending most of their time, and it makes it genuinely easy for someone to discover a product they like and actually complete the purchase without friction or frustration.

This is the first of three concepts we'll be presenting, and we're really looking for your feedback before we refine things and move forward. I'm happy to walk through any of this in more detail or answer any questions you might have about the direction or individual choices we made.
